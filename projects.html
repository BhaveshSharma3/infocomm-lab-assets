Research

Our research spans theory, algorithms, and experimental systems in modern wireless networks.

Core Research Areas

  • Wireless LANs
  • Wireless Communications
  • Information Theory
  • Wireless Energy Harvesting
  • IoT Networks
  • Wireless Sensing

Cell-free Massive MIMO

Cell-Free Massive Multiple-Input Multiple-Output (CF-mMIMO) is a distributed wireless communication technology that eliminates fixed cell boundaries found in traditional cellular networks.

In CF-mMIMO systems, a large number of geographically distributed, low-power access points jointly serve all users using the same time–frequency resources under centralized coordination.

Massive MIMO Communication Networks

Massive MIMO employs a large number of antennas at base stations to significantly improve spectral efficiency, reliability, and throughput.

It enables advanced techniques such as beamforming and spatial multiplexing and is a key enabling technology for 5G and beyond wireless networks.

Wireless Powered Communication Networks (WPCN)

WPCNs enable wireless devices to harvest energy from radio frequency (RF) signals, allowing energy-constrained devices to operate perpetually without battery replacement.

This paradigm is particularly useful for IoT sensors and wireless sensor networks.

Network Coding and Information Theory

Network information theory studies the fundamental limits of information flow in networks. Network coding enables intermediate nodes to perform coding operations instead of simple forwarding.

This approach can achieve capacity bounds in many network settings.

Intelligent Reflecting Surfaces (IRS)

IRS technology enhances wireless performance by intelligently reconfiguring the propagation environment using programmable reflective elements.

IRS enables passive beamforming, improving coverage, capacity, and energy efficiency.

Backscatter Communication

Backscatter communication allows ultra-low-power devices to transmit data by reflecting existing RF signals instead of generating their own carriers.

This makes it highly suitable for battery-free IoT and sensing applications.
